Stadium

King Salman International Stadium

The King Salman International Stadium in Riyadh will host the 2034 FIFA World Cup opening and final, with a 92,000-seat capacity. Opening in 2029, it will later become home to the Saudi national team, aligning with Saudi Vision 2030.

Neom Stadium

NEOM Stadium, part of Saudi Arabia's NEOM project, is a futuristic sports venue designed to host world-class events. Combining innovative architecture with sustainability, it aligns with Saudi Vision 2030.

Murabba Stadium

Featuring artificial canyons and rock structures, this stadium's design draws inspiration from Saudi Arabia's natural landscapes.

Prince Mohammed bin Salman Stadium

Situated on a cliff, this stadium features a unique design that integrates with the natural landscape, offering spectators breathtaking views

King Abdullah Sports City Stadium

An existing venue with a capacity of 62,345, it will be one of the primary stadiums in Jeddah for the World Cup

Aramco Stadium

47,000-seat venue with a whirlpool-inspired design reflecting maritime heritage. Opening in 2026, it will host 2034 FIFA World Cup matches and serve as Al-Qadsiah FC's home. The project aligns with Saudi Vision 2030.

Roshn Stadium

40,000-seat venue designed to host major sporting events, including matches for the 2034 FIFA World Cup. Its innovative design blends modern architecture with local culture and aims to align with Saudi Vision 2030.
